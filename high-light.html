Marco Ciceri
High Light

Light installation
by Marco Ciceri and Axel Schoterman

Format: x3 High resolution screens/ x3 projectors

"High Light" expresses the interplay of time and light. At its core, the installation showcases a dialogue between two distinct sources of illumination: the timeless flicker of a candle and its replication in the contemporary glow of an LED sculpture.

Watching a flame has long been a common meditative experience - in this piece, the viewer is invited to use that practice to reflect on the contrast and harmony of these two disparate light sources. As the candle flickers and diminishes in brightness, its digital counterpart follows, echoing the rhythmic cadence of the fire's illumination.

The technological dissonance between the candle and the LED sculpture prompts reflection on the evolution of human innovation and its relationship to ancient traditions. While through their coexistence within the installation, the passage of time becomes perceivable, as observers witness the gradual transformation of light from both the natural flame and the artificial LED.

In essence, "High Light" serves as a visual meditation on the enduring presence of light across time and technologies, bridging the gap between past and present while allowing the viewer to immerse themselves in a reverie of contrasts and connections.

Marco Ciceri

Marco Ciceri is an Italian visual artist, based in Berlin. Renowned for his immersive live shows and groundbreaking installations, he masterfully blends light and video into breathtaking experiences. With a five-year tenure at the avant-garde collective Pfadfinderei, preceded by a pivotal role at the internationally acclaimed Marshmallow Laser Feast in London, Marco’s career trajectory is marked by exceptional collaboration and innovation. Armed with a design and arts degree from the University of Bolzano in Italy, he seamlessly integrates software-driven automation and physical mechanics into his animations, crafting intricate, fluid worlds. Marco’s work is a testament to his relentless pursuit of aesthetic perfection, where multiple mediums converge to create unparalleled visual spectacles.

Axel Schoterman

Axel Schoterman is a visual artist and art director based in Berlin. His work focuses on computer-generated images and films for live A/V performance, music videos, video installations and CGI art and design. Previously art director at Builders Club in London and currently working as a freelancer in Berlin
