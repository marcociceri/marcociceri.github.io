Marta De Pascalis

Marta De Pascalis’ sonic world acts as an uncanny translator that freezes and expands emotions, conveying them into unique soundscapes. Her solo works employ analog, fm synthesis, and a tape-loop system, whereby she carves waveforms to shape cathartic sound bodies. She has performed at several festivals and venues, notably Berlin Atonal, Museo Reina Sofia, Berghain, Volksbühne, Café Oto, Mutek Festival.
Her music has been published on The Tapeworm, Morphine Records, Canti Magnetici, Caterina Barbieri’s label light-years and Mute Records.

Marco Ciceri

Marco Ciceri is a Berlin-based Italian media artist specializing in immersive audio-visual installations and performances. He studied design and art at the Free University of Bozen/Bolzano, where his thesis explored the contrast between live and mediated reality. After collaborating with Marshmallow Laser Feast in 2014, he joined Berlin's Pfadfinderei studio in 2015, creating visuals for renowned venues and festivals.
Ciceri has a long-standing partnership with musician Grand River, producing audio-video shows that merge light, music, and visuals, showcased at venues like Milan’s Triennale Museum and London’s Barbican Center. He has also worked independently, contributing installations to festivals such as Terraforma and Mutek. Since 2020, he has collaborated with Alessandro Cortini, developing visual content for live performances, including “Nati Infiniti,” which premiered at Berlin Atonal in 2023.
Known for its “flowing esthetic,” Ciceri integrates generative software and motorised mechanics to create dynamic, detailed animations.
