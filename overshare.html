Marco Ciceri
Overshare

Performer, coreographer: Caterina Politi
Visual art: Marco Ciceri
Music: Martina Bertoni

Duration: 50 min

Overshare is a performance work by visual artist Marco Ciceri and dancer-choreographer Caterina Politi that dives into the rituals of self-exposure in the age of social media. What does it mean to perform yourself, not on stage, but every day, for an invisible audience? Through movement, image, and the tension between presence and its digital double, Overshare explores the new choreographies of visibility that shape how we inhabit our bodies and our lives. The piece begins where the screen ends: in the breath, the gesture, the moment before and after the post.
Drawing from the term’s evolution of “share” from cutting/dividing to today’s constant broadcasting. Our research asks when intimacy turns into oversharing, and why screens and anonymity can make communication easier than face-to-face feedback. On stage, movement and real-time visuals construct “The Fragmented Self”: the tension between raw private reality and curated digital persona. Cameras and macro lenses become metaphors of division, splitting perception into three simultaneous viewpoints: intimate close-ups (private detail), live projections (public/fragmented self), and the performer’s body as witnessed in the room. Creation starts from the body, building a physical vocabulary through improvisation, choreography, and technological experimentation; visual artist Marco operates the live system on stage as an active participant.
The performance aims to embody the numb search for the “ideal post” and the invasive closeness of the zoom, we ask how little our "shares" truly reveal about us, and how much we actually grasp of someone's story from those fragments alone. Staged in temporary, accessible spaces, we want to speak especially to young people navigating social anxiety, alienation, and identity-making online, offering recognition rather than moral judgment. As Sherry Turkle noted in the early 2010s, “I share, therefore I am,” and the piece tests whether this digital existence is enough. We examine how identity formation is shaped by self-presentation, role models, social comparison, and the omnipresent online audience, until authenticity itself risks becoming an artificial performance. The environment forces a final question: do we surrender to standard expectations and homogenise ourselves for social media, or artificially construct identity just to fit in, and what is our true aim in the act of “sharing”?
Marco Ciceri is an artist and researcher. His multidisciplinary practice encompasses live performance, installation, physical artwork, and photography. He studied design and art at the Free University of Bozen/Bolzano, where his thesis explored the contrast between live and mediated reality. After collaborating with Marshmallow Laser Feast in 2014, he spent five years at Berlin’s Pfadfinderei studio (2015–2020). Now an independent artist, he collaborated with musicians including Alessandro Cortini and Grand River, and presented his work at Treinnale Milano, Barbican Center and festivals like CTM, Atonal, STEGI.RADIO Takeover and MUTEK. In his work, he expresses through a “flowing esthetic” stories that explore the interaction between technology and nature.
Caterina is a Berlin-based freelance dancer and performer, born in Italy in 1994. She trained at Balletto di Toscana in Florence.She joined after her study Antitesi(IT) and Junior Balletto di Toscana(IT). Later working with Spellbound Contemporary Ballet performing internationally in works by choreographers M. Astolfi, M. Morau and S. Jijia.Since 2022, she is freelancer collaborating with Aerites Dance Company(GR) for the production “the House of Trouble” 2023 (Commissioned & Produced by Onassis Stegi) and “History” 2025, Hungry Sharks (AT), Wang Ramirez (FR) and Frantics Dance Company(DE). Additionally she is part of private event and site specific performance such as Dance MyS+eries/ Season 2 and 4 June 2023, May 2025, in Eleusis GR (artistic director Patricia Apergi, with Aerites dance company), production of 2023 Eleusis European Capital of Culture and U(R)TOPIAS Academy of Choreography. She has taught at numerous academies, including U(R)TOPIAS Academy of Choreography(Greece), D.A.F. Dance Arts Faculty(Italy), Teatro dell’Opera di Roma (Italy), CDSH (Hamburg), and Ukrainian Ballet Academy (Italy) and other.
